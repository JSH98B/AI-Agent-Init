# Lesson 1: What is AI?

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the history of AI and key milestones
- Distinguish between narrow AI and general AI
- Explain why AI is transformative right now

## Key Concepts

### Brief History
- **1950s**: Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test; "Can machines think?"
- **1956**: "Artificial Intelligence" coined at Dartmouth Conference
- **1980s**: Expert systems boom — hand-coded rules for specific domains
- **1990s–2000s**: Machine learning rises; statistical models replace hand-crafted rules
- **2012**: Deep learning breakthrough — AlexNet wins ImageNet by a massive margin
- **2017**: "Attention Is All You Need" — the Transformer paper changes everything
- **2022–present**: ChatGPT, GPT-4, Claude, Gemini — LLMs go mainstream

### Narrow AI vs. General AI
| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Narrow AI (ANI) | Excels at ONE specific task | Chess engines, spam filters, image classifiers |
| General AI (AGI) | Matches human-level reasoning across ALL tasks | Theoretical — doesn't exist yet |
| Superintelligence (ASI) | Surpasses human intelligence in every domain | Hypothetical future |

Everything you'll use day-to-day (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) is **narrow AI** — incredibly capable within their domain, but not general reasoners.

### Why Now?
Three forces converged:
1. **Data** — The internet created petabytes of text to train on
2. **Compute** — GPUs made matrix math fast enough to train billion-parameter models
3. **Algorithms** — Transformers unlocked efficient parallel training at scale

## Key Takeaways
- AI ≠ magic. It's pattern matching at massive scale.
- We're in the narrow AI era — powerful but specialized
- The Transformer architecture (2017) is the foundation of every major LLM today
